Present Simple Tense

The Present Simple Tense is used to describe actions that happen regularly or for habitual actions, general truths, and permanent states. It is one of the most commonly used tenses in English. This tense is formed using the base form of the verb for plural subjects and "he/she/it" subjects with an additional “-s” or “-es”.

For example:

  • I eat breakfast every day. (मैं हर दिन नाश्ता करता हूँ।)
  • She plays tennis on weekends. (वह सप्ताहांत में टेनिस खेलती है।)

Sentence Structure and Formula:

In affirmative sentences, the formula is:
Subject + verb + Object
(for "I", "we", "you", "they") :- Don't Use s/es Example: I read books. (मैं किताबें पढ़ता हूँ।)

For third-person singular (he, she, it), the formula is:
Subject + verb with "s" or "es"
Example: She reads books. (वह किताबें पढ़ती है।)

Affirmative Sentences (सकारात्मक वाक्य)

Affirmative sentences in the Present Simple Tense express a fact or habitual action in a positive manner.

Examples:

  • He walks to school every day. (वह हर दिन स्कूल जाता है।)
  • We watch TV at night. (हम रात में टीवी देखते हैं।)

Interrogative Sentences (Present Simple)
Explanation:
In Present Simple, interrogative sentences are formed by placing the helping verb "do" or "does" before the subject followed by the base form of the main verb.
वर्तमान साधारण काल में प्रश्नवाचक वाक्य "do" या "does" सहायक क्रिया को विषय से पहले रखकर बनाए जाते हैं, इसके बाद मुख्य क्रिया का आधार रूप आता है।
Formula:
Do/Does + Subject + Verb (base form) + Object?
क्या + कर्ता + क्रिया (मूल रूप) + कर्म?
